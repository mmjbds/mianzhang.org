Downgrade

Uncertain perception should narrow the claim before it triggers action.

A VLA system may classify, describe, plan, and act in one chain. The public boundary asks which part has evidence and where uncertainty must stop action.

If the perception evidence is uncertain, the public claim should route to review, stop, or wording that exactly matches the validated envelope.

Permission

Perception confidence is not action permission.

The system may be allowed to inspect or describe a scene without being allowed to move a robot, alter a workflow, or trigger a production effect.

That distinction is central to the robot/vision downgrade rule.

FAQ

What is a VLA downgrade rule?

It is a rule that routes uncertain visual evidence to review, stop, or narrower wording instead of action permission.

FAQ

Why is this needed?

Because a chain from image to language to action can hide the moment where evidence stopped supporting permission.
