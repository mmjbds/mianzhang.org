Weekly Evidence Map - 2026-05-21

The first public week is now a traceable evidence map.

Wisdom Science is not a slogan about larger models. It is a claim discipline: every architecture, recovery step, and public claim should connect back to evidence, boundaries, replay, and failure-aware repair.

Evidence Map Paper Map

What Changed This Week

From public posts to an auditable architecture.

The first week of public rollout established a coherent route through the work: evidence gates, embodied recovery, robust perception, reflexive world models, and claim-to-replay evidence contracts. Each note is useful alone, but the stronger point is their joint boundary: reliability is a system property after failure.

The evidence map is therefore not a marketing timeline. It is a public ledger of what can be claimed, what remains bounded, and which mechanisms must be replayable before a result is treated as reliable action.

Supported Boundaries

Strong claims need explicit edges.

The public release is intentionally bounded. The current artifacts support a research program around longitudinal evaluation, recovery after failure, evidence ledgers, robust perception under disturbance, and reflexive world-model testing.

They do not claim universal autonomy, detector or tracker state of the art, real-robot deployment proof, medical validity, financial advice, or autonomous enforcement. The boundary is part of the contribution: a reliable architecture must know where it stops.

Review Ledger

The system is bounded by what can be traced.

If an action, hypothesis, or repair sequence cannot be logged, replayed, and cross-examined, the architecture should lower autonomy. This is the shared constraint behind the week: evidence is not decoration around a claim. It is the control surface of the system.

  • Claims must identify their evidence path and assumption boundary.
  • Failures must remain recoverable samples, not disappear into aggregate metrics.
  • World-model results must stay tied to replayable conditions and counterfactuals.
  • Public statements must separate supported evidence from future work.
