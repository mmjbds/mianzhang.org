A useful weekly digest is not a victory report.

Artifact: Boundary-First Weekly Evidence Digest. A public digest should say what changed: which claims strengthened, which narrowed, which downgraded, and which still lack evidence.

Artifact

Evidence work updates boundaries.

This digest records strengthened, unchanged, downgraded, and still-unproven public claims. A narrowed claim can be progress when it makes the public record harder to misunderstand.

This page does not treat upload status, DOI presence, page count, or visual polish as validation.

Weekly Boundary

A digest should show what did not get stronger.

If every weekly update sounds stronger, the digest may be performing confidence instead of tracking evidence. Public evidence work needs room for unchanged, downgraded, and not-yet-proven states.

That makes the record less flashy, but more useful for readers who need to know what can be cited and what should wait.

Evidence Status

Upload status is not validation.

An uploaded page or DOI record can help with citation and access. It does not automatically prove the claim, close the evidence gap, or upgrade candidate evidence into validation.

The weekly digest keeps evidence status and distribution status separate.

Challenge Surface

Find any line that hides limits in the footnotes.

A useful challenge points to a claim whose limitations appear only as a footnote, afterthought, or vague caveat instead of a visible boundary.

The repair is to move the boundary into the main claim surface and update the status row.
