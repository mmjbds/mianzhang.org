Public Release - 2026-07-07

An independent other is not a supporter; it is a route that changes the failure distribution.

Artifact: independent_other_law_note_v1. This note separates emotional support from independent correction: a person counts as an independent other only when their signal changes the observable failure pattern of a decision system.

Artifact

A supporter can stabilize mood without changing the failure mechanism.

Support is not the same as independent correction. A relationship, partnership, mentor loop, or founder review process becomes technically relevant only when it changes the distribution of mistakes the actor would otherwise keep repeating.

Decision Route

The evidence object is the change in failures, not the comfort of the interaction.

The packet records the failure mode, the blind spot, the independent signal, and the decision record that changed after the signal entered the system. Without this route, advice can become style preference, loyalty theater, or retrospective storytelling.

Review

An independent other must survive disagreement and still update the boundary.

For founders, teams, partnerships, and high-stakes decisions, the test is not whether another person agrees. The test is whether their disagreement remains traceable enough to alter the next decision boundary and reduce a repeated failure class.
