Public Release - 2026-07-19

A financial AI that always answers may not know when to stop.

Artifact: financial_ai_refusal_gate_v1. This note separates a visible recommendation from an authorized financial action: the evidence date, missing evidence, uncertainty, refusal trigger, and prohibited action have to remain explicit before a workflow can move money or take risk.

claim evidence_asof missing_evidence uncertainty refusal_trigger prohibited_action boundary_update review_status
Evidence Map Registries Boundaries Counterexamples

Artifact

A recommendation is not an authorization.

Financial AI can produce a fluent answer from stale prices, partial information, or an unverified identity claim. The review object asks what evidence is current, what is missing, and which action remains prohibited before the output receives additional authority.

Refusal

The stop condition has to exist before the loss does.

A refusal trigger is not a generic warning label. It is a recorded condition that blocks a transfer, trade, or risk increase when evidence is stale, uncertainty is material, or a required confirmation is absent.

Review

If a system cannot name the prohibited action, its boundary is incomplete.

A useful challenge should identify a missing field, an unsupported causal link, a stale evidence timestamp, or a condition under which the proposed control fails. The protocol does not certify a product or guarantee a financial outcome.
