Public Release - 2026-07-22

AI time savings are not net gains until workload and recovery debt are counted.

Artifact: efficiency_rebound_ledger_v1. This workforce application note records where claimed AI time savings go after deployment. Its minimum accounting rule is net_gain = time_saved - workload_added - recovery_debt; throughput alone does not establish a net benefit.

Application

A productivity claim is incomplete until the allocation is visible.

This note applies the Efficiency Rebound Ledger published on 2026-07-17 to workforce decisions. It keeps time saved separate from work added, output-floor shifts, human recovery time, and the recipient of the benefit. Reusing the same artifact makes this an application note, not a second independent protocol.

Accounting

Efficiency and job security can move in opposite directions.

The ledger does not treat throughput as a moral property or an employment guarantee. It asks whether the measured savings reduce workload, raise targets, shorten staffing, or create a faster response obligation. The minimum equation is net_gain = time_saved - workload_added - recovery_debt.

Boundary

The ledger records allocation; it does not infer a layoff cause.

A useful review identifies the comparison window, added work, changed output floor, protected recovery time, accountable boundary owner, and final benefit recipient. This object does not reduce any company's staffing decision to AI alone, issue a legal conclusion, or promise an individual income outcome.

1

time_saved

The measured task time or attention budget reduced under a defined comparison window.

2

workload_added

The review, coordination, monitoring, cleanup, or new task load introduced with the tool.

3

output_floor_shift

The change in expected baseline output after the claimed efficiency becomes normal.

4

human_recovery_time

The time people actually recover after added work and recovery debt are deducted.

5

boundary_owner

The accountable person or team that can limit workload rebound and revise the operating boundary.

6

benefit_recipient

The person, team, customer, or organization that ultimately captures the measured gain.
