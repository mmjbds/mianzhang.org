More agents do not create more independent observers when they share the same blind spot.

Artifact: independent_other_review_packet_v1. This note separates nominal observers from effective independent evidence and defines the fields required to review a claim before automation turns a shared blind spot into a workflow.

Artifact

Nominal observers are not the same as independent evidence.

A larger room, a larger committee, or a larger agent swarm can still collapse into one effective observer when residuals are highly correlated. A compact approximation is N_eff = N / (1 + (N - 1) rho). The field that matters is not headcount; it is the non-redundant correction signal.

Evidence Type

Benchmarks, demos, road tests, and orchestration are evidence types, not deployment proof.

A benchmark is useful evidence. It is not the world. A robot demo records a task under specific conditions; it is not a reliability distribution. A road-test video is not a safety case. An agent workflow is not automatically an accountable decision system.

Review

The independent other is the correction source that can make a claim narrower.

The review packet asks who can expose the blind spot, name the residual signal, open the counterexample route, and force the boundary update. Without those fields, AI governance, agent orchestration, and benchmark narratives can scale the same error.
